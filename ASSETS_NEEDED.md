# Council of Chalcedon - Required Art Assets

## Overview

All images should be placed in the `game/images/` directory structure. Recommended resolution: **1920x1080** for backgrounds, **300x500** minimum for character sprites.

**Historical Context**: The Council of Chalcedon took place in 451 AD in the city of Chalcedon (modern-day Kadıköy, Istanbul). It was the Fourth Ecumenical Council, attended by over 500 bishops. The council defined that Jesus Christ has two natures (divine and human) united in one person.

### Roman Legates (Gold theme - #c9a227)

These are the representatives sent by Pope Leo I of Rome. They carried his famous "Tome" (letter) and spoke with his authority. Rome was the most prestigious see in Western Christianity.

**Filename:** `paschasinus.png`
**Character:** Paschasinus, Bishop of Lilybaeum (Sicily)
**Who he was:** The lead Roman legate - Pope Leo's chief representative and most aggressive enforcer at the council. He read the charges against Dioscorus and pronounced the sentence of deposition.
**Role:** Lead accuser, voice of papal authority
**Personality:** Stern, commanding, unwavering
**Age:** Late 50s
**Ethnicity:** Italian/Roman - olive skin, Mediterranean features
**Face shape:** Square jaw, prominent Roman nose (aquiline), strong brow ridge
**Hair:** Gray, receding hairline, tonsured (shaved crown - typical of Western clergy)
**Facial hair:** Clean-shaven or very short gray stubble
**Distinguishing features:** Deep-set eyes, pronounced forehead wrinkles, stern frown lines between brows
**Expression:** Stern and commanding - pursed lips, narrowed eyes, disapproving gaze
**Pose:** Standing upright, one hand raised in accusation or holding a scroll
**Vestments:** Western bishop - white alb, gold chasuble, pallium (white wool band with crosses)

### Alexandrian Faction (Dark Red theme - #8b0000)

Alexandria (Egypt) was one of the most powerful Christian centers. Its theology emphasized Christ's divine nature. Dioscorus led the "Robber Council" of 449 that the Council of Chalcedon was called to overturn.

**Filename:** `dioscorus.png`
**Character:** Dioscorus, Patriarch of Alexandria
**Who he was:** The powerful patriarch of Egypt, second only to Rome in prestige. He led the "Robber Council" of 449 where Flavian was beaten to death. Now he is the accused, stripped of his seat and facing judgment.
**Role:** The antagonist - proud, defiant, refusing to submit
**Personality:** Arrogant, imperious, unyielding even in defeat
**Age:** Mid 60s
**Ethnicity:** Egyptian/Coptic - dark brown skin, North African features (IMPORTANT: visibly darker than Greek or Roman characters)
**Face shape:** Broad face, heavy jowls, thick neck
**Hair:** White/silver, balding on top, some hair remaining on sides
**Facial hair:** Full white beard, long and unkempt (Eastern patriarch style)
**Distinguishing features:** Heavy-lidded eyes, thick dark eyebrows (still dark despite white hair), deep nasolabial folds, age spots on forehead, prominent ears
**Expression:** Proud and contemptuous - chin raised, looking down his nose, slight sneer
**Pose:** Arms crossed or hands gripping chair arms, defensive but proud posture
**Vestments:** Eastern patriarch - ornate gold and red phelonion (chasuble), omophorion (decorated stole), but vestments appear slightly disheveled (he's been stripped of honor)

### Imperial Figures (Purple theme - #9932cc)

The Emperor and Empress called the council and enforced its decisions. Purple was the imperial color - only the Emperor could wear it.

**Filename:** `marcian.png`
**Character:** Emperor Marcian (Flavius Marcianus)
**Who he was:** Roman Emperor of the East (450-457 AD). A former soldier who became emperor by marrying Pulcheria. He was about 60 years old at the council. He personally attended Session VI to ratify the Definition of Faith.
**Role:** Imperial authority - he called the council and enforced order
**Personality:** Military bearing, practical, no-nonsense
**Historical note:** He refused to pay tribute to Attila the Hun, saying "I have gold for my friends, steel for my enemies."
**Age:** About 60
**Ethnicity:** Thracian/Roman - weathered tan skin from military campaigns
**Face shape:** Rugged, squared, strong jawline - a soldier's face
**Hair:** Silver-white, short military cut, receding significantly
**Facial hair:** Short, neat gray beard trimmed close (military style, not long like clergy)
**Distinguishing features:** Battle scars (small scar on cheek or brow), sun-weathered skin with deep wrinkles, strong nose (possibly broken once), piercing authoritative eyes, thick neck
**Expression:** Commanding and impatient - stern but not cruel, the look of a man who wants results
**Pose:** Standing tall with military posture, possibly hand on sword hilt or arms crossed
**Clothing:** Imperial purple robe over military-style tunic, golden diadem/crown, possibly visible armor elements (pauldrons, breastplate edge), red military cloak

---

**Filename:** `pulcheria.png`
**Character:** Empress Pulcheria (Aelia Pulcheria Augusta)
**Who she was:** One of the most remarkable women in Roman history. Sister of the previous emperor Theodosius II, she had taken a vow of virginity at age 15 and essentially ruled the empire for her weak brother. When he died, she became empress and married Marcian (a nominal marriage - she kept her vow of virginity). She was the driving force behind calling Chalcedon.
**Role:** Champion of orthodoxy, the spiritual force behind the council
**Personality:** Pious, strong-willed, intelligent, devoted to the Virgin Mary
**Historical note:** She built many churches and was venerated as a saint
**Age:** Early 50s
**Ethnicity:** Greek/Roman - pale complexion (she lived a sheltered, pious life indoors)
**Face shape:** Refined, elegant, heart-shaped face
**Hair:** Dark brown with gray, completely hidden under imperial veil/headdress
**Distinguishing features:** Serene but penetrating eyes, high cheekbones, thin arched eyebrows, small delicate mouth, no jewelry except religious items (cross, icon), very modest makeup despite rank
**Expression:** Serene and devout - gentle smile, eyes looking slightly upward (heavenward), peaceful but resolute
**Pose:** Hands folded in prayer, or holding a small cross or icon
**Clothing:** Imperial purple robes but more modest than typical empress (high neckline, long sleeves), elaborate Byzantine crown/headdress with veil, pearl earrings, prominent pectoral cross

### Other Major Figures

**Filename:** `eusebius.png`
**Character:** Eusebius, Bishop of Dorylaeum
**Who he was:** The man who started it all. He was the one who formally accused the monk Eutyches of heresy in 448 AD, triggering the whole crisis. At the Robber Council of 449, Dioscorus had him condemned. Now at Chalcedon, he's vindicated.
**Role:** The accuser - presents testimony against Eutyches and Dioscorus
**Color:** Green (#2e8b57)
**Personality:** Zealous, persistent, fiery, won't back down
**Age:** Late 40s
**Ethnicity:** Anatolian Greek - olive skin, dark features
**Face shape:** Angular, sharp features, pointed chin
**Hair:** Black with some gray, thick and slightly wild/unkempt, Eastern-style (no tonsure)
**Facial hair:** Full black beard, somewhat bushy and untrimmed
**Distinguishing features:** Intense dark eyes (almost burning), thick eyebrows that meet in the middle, sharp cheekbones, animated features, gesticulates when speaking
**Expression:** Intense and passionate - eyes wide, leaning forward, the look of a zealot who has finally been proven right
**Pose:** Pointing accusingly, or gesturing emphatically with both hands, body language urgent
**Vestments:** Eastern bishop - green and gold phelonion, simpler than patriarchs, omophorion

**Filename:** `theodoretus.png`
**Character:** Theodoretus, Bishop of Cyrrhus (Syria)
**Who he was:** A brilliant theologian and historian, but controversial. He had been a friend and defender of Nestorius (condemned as a heretic at Ephesus 431). He also wrote against Cyril of Alexandria. Dioscorus had him condemned at the Robber Council. At Chalcedon, he was restored - but only after publicly anathematizing Nestorius.
**Role:** The controversial figure who must prove his orthodoxy
**Color:** Olive (#6b8e23)
**Personality:** Scholarly, cautious, slightly defensive, intellectual
**Age:** About 65 (oldest of the major figures)
**Ethnicity:** Syrian - medium brown skin, Semitic features (DISTINCT from Greek characters)
**Face shape:** Long, narrow face, gaunt cheeks
**Hair:** Completely white, thin and wispy, balding significantly
**Facial hair:** Long thin white beard, neatly groomed (scholar's beard)
**Distinguishing features:** Deep-set tired eyes (from years of study), spectacles or squinting expression (poor eyesight from reading), prominent nose with slight hook, liver spots on temples, stooped posture from years hunched over manuscripts
**Expression:** Weary but dignified - the look of a man tired of defending himself, resigned but not defeated
**Pose:** Holding a scroll or book, slightly hunched, perhaps one hand raised in explanation
**Vestments:** Eastern bishop - olive/earth-toned phelonion, simple but dignified, ink stains on fingers (a writer's hands)

### Imperial Officials (Purple theme - #9932cc)

**Filename:** `florentius.png`
**Character:** Florentius, Patrician and Praetorian Prefect of the East
**Who he was:** The senior imperial commissioner at the council. As Praetorian Prefect, he was the highest-ranking civil official in the Eastern Empire after the Emperor himself. He and the other commissioners (including the Master of Soldiers and senators) represented imperial authority and kept order during proceedings.
**Role:** Lead imperial commissioner - asks questions, demands clarity, announces decisions
**Color:** Purple (#9932cc) - same as imperial figures
**Personality:** Stern, efficient, impatient with theological squabbling, focused on results
**Age:** Mid 50s
**Ethnicity:** Roman aristocrat - pale complexion (indoor bureaucratic life), refined features
**Face shape:** Long, patrician face, high cheekbones, thin lips
**Hair:** Gray, short and neatly trimmed in Roman style, receding slightly
**Facial hair:** Clean-shaven (Roman officials were typically clean-shaven, unlike Eastern clergy)
**Distinguishing features:** Sharp, intelligent eyes, prominent nose, thin arched eyebrows, slight frown lines from constant evaluation, ink-stained fingers (a man of documents)
**Expression:** Stern and evaluating - the look of a judge weighing evidence, slightly impatient
**Pose:** Standing with a scroll or tablet, or seated with hands on a table, authoritative posture
**Clothing:** NOT vestments - secular Roman official dress: white toga praetexta with purple border (his rank), or rich dalmatic tunic in purple/white, possibly a chlamys (cloak) with imperial fibula, no religious symbols - he represents the Emperor, not the Church

## Art Style Recommendations

### Historical Period
- **Era**: 5th century Byzantine/Late Roman
- **Clothing**: Episcopal vestments (omophorion, phelonion), imperial purple, Roman senatorial togas
- **Architecture**: Byzantine basilica style, columns, mosaics

### Color Palette by Faction

| Faction | Primary Color | Hex Code | Meaning |
|---------|--------------|----------|---------|
| Roman Legates | Gold | #c9a227 | Papal authority |
| Alexandria | Dark Red | #8b0000 | Opposition/antagonist |
| Constantinople | Royal Blue | #4169e1 | Eastern church |
| Imperial | Purple | #9932cc | Imperial power |
| Other Eastern | Steel Blue | #4682b4 | Neutral Eastern bishops |

### Suggested Art Styles
1. **Byzantine Iconographic** - Stylized, gold backgrounds, flat perspective
2. **Realistic Historical** - Painted realistic style
3. **Anime/VN Style** - Modern visual novel aesthetic with historical costumes
